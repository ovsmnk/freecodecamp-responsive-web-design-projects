Isaac Newton

English mathematician, physicist, astronomer, alchemist, theologian and author

Information about Isaac Newton[1]

Isaac Newton was an English physicist and mathematician who was the culminating figure of the Scientific Revolution of the 17th century.

In optics, his discovery of the composition of white light integrated the phenomena of colours into the science of light and laid the foundation for modern physical optics.

In mechanics, his three laws of motion, the basic principles of modern physics, resulted in the formulation of the law of universal gravitation.

In mathematics, he was the original discoverer of the infinitesimal calculus. Newton’s Philosophiae Naturalis Principia Mathematica (Mathematical Principles of Natural Philosophy, 1687) was one of the most important single works in the history of modern science.

Time line of Isaac Newton's life[3]

  1. 1643

    Birth

    Isaac Newton was born in Woolsthorpe, Lincolnshire, England.

  2. 1661

    Enters Trinity College, Cambridge

    Newton enrolled at Trinity College, Cambridge, where he studied mathematics, physics, and astronomy.

  3. 1665

    First scientific work

    Newton began his studies in optics, developing his theories on light and color that would become his first major scientific work.

  4. 1687

    Principia Mathematica

    Newton published his book "Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica," which outlined his laws of motion and universal gravitation.

  5. 1696

    Warden of the Royal Mint

    Newton was appointed as Warden of the Royal Mint, where he worked to combat counterfeiting and reform the currency.

  6. 1703

    President of the Royal Society

    Newton was elected as President of the Royal Society, a position he held until his death.

  7. 1727

    Death

    Isaac Newton died at the age of 84 in London, England.
